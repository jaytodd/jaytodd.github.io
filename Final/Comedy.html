Comedy Shows

The following TV shows are some of the best comedy TV shows avaliable to watch. For this category I will be looking at how well jokes land and how watching the show makes you feel. A good comedy should relate with its audience and make them feel happier after watching.

#1 Seinfeld

Sienfeld Is a legendary show written by Jerry Sienfeld. Every episode of Seinfeld is exceptionally clever and the characters make the comedy halarious. The show manages to make simple things such as washing hands or waiting in line very funny through observational humor. Jerry Seinfeld and Larry David are masters of observational humor and did a good job making people laugh about every day inconveniences.


#2 Friends

Frends is a beloved show that stars a loveable cast of characters who are all now extremely famous. Friends is often considered one of the best comedies which is understandable due to the fact that it makes people feel good after watching it. The characters are extremely quirky and they all bring some form of comedy to the table whether it be idiocy, cluelessness, or sassyness. I have not watched much Friends, but it can't be ignored as one of the best comedy shows.


#3 The Office

The Office is one of the most watched sitcoms, especially today. The office is very clever and relatable, taking place in a common office, and documenting the lives and relationships of the employees. The Office has a very uniqe style of humor that makes it stand out as an amazing comedy.


#4 Curb Your Enthusiasm,

Larry David's Curb Your Enthusiasm is one of my personal favorites and demonstrates the comedic prowess of Larry David. In this show, Larry David lives every day life and ends up having an existnetial battle with every social rule that he is forced to follow while interacting in the world. Curb Your Enthusiasm is a very clever show that consistantly keeps me laughing.


Honorable Mention: Arrested Development

Arrested Development Is a lighthearted comedy that is extremely funny mostly due to how crazy the main characters are and the situations they get themselves into. Everyone is an exaggerated stereotype of a rich family member. With a large fortune to be divided up eventually, the Bluth Family is constantly bickering and plotting ways to get themselves into a lucrative will.
